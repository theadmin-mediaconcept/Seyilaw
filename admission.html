The Process

Step-by-Step
Admission Journey

Whether applying for the first time or re-sitting, we walk you through each stage clearly and confidently.
01
JAMB Registration & UTME
The first step is registering with JAMB (Joint Admissions and Matriculation Board). We handle your full registration — NIN verification, profile creation, biometric capture and exam center selection.
What We Help With
  • NIN and JAMB profile creation
  • Subject combination guidance
  • JAMB mock exam registration
  • UTME result checking
02
Choosing Your Institution & Course
After UTME, select your preferred institution and course. We guide you on cut-off marks, course requirements and which schools accept your score to maximize your chances.
What We Help With
  • Understanding cut-off marks
  • First & second choice institution
  • Course eligibility & requirements
  • Change of institution/course
03
Post-UTME & Screening
Most institutions conduct their own Post-UTME screening. We help you register, access the school portal, download your exam slip and prepare for the screening exam or interview.
What We Help With
  • Post-UTME form registration
  • Portal access & navigation
  • Exam slip printing
  • Screening preparation tips
04
Checking Admission Status
Once results are out, you need to check your JAMB CAPS portal for admission status. We monitor this for you, notify you and guide you on accepting your admission promptly.
What We Help With
  • JAMB CAPS portal check
  • Institution admission portal
  • Admission acceptance steps
  • Rejection re-application guide
05
Acceptance Fee Payment
After admission, you will need to pay the acceptance fee on your school's portal. We process this payment accurately, confirm your receipt and ensure your school record is updated.
What We Help With
  • Acceptance fee portal payment
  • Payment receipt printing
  • Confirming payment status
  • School portal profile setup
06
Clearance & Registration
The final stage involves document clearance at your institution. We help you prepare and organize all required documents and guide you through the full registration and matriculation process.
What We Help With
  • Document checklist preparation
  • O'Level result verification
  • Course & unit registration
  • Student ID & matriculation

Common Questions

Admission FAQs

Answers to the questions students ask us most. Still confused? Come see us!
What is JAMB and when should I register?
+

JAMB (Joint Admissions and Matriculation Board) is Nigeria's national body that conducts the UTME for entry into tertiary institutions. Registration usually opens between January and March each year. You should come to us as soon as registration opens to avoid long queues and website crashes.

What score do I need to gain admission?
+

The minimum score varies by institution and course. Federal universities typically require 180+, state universities 160+, and polytechnics 150+. However, competitive courses like Medicine, Law and Engineering require much higher scores (240–280+). We advise students on what score they need for their specific institution and course choice.

I missed admission last year. Can I still apply?
+

Absolutely. You can re-sit JAMB as many times as needed. There is no limit to how many times you can write JAMB. We help many candidates who are on their second or third attempt and have helped them successfully gain admission.

What happens if I don't get admission after Post-UTME?
+

If you don't get admission after Post-UTME, you have several options: use the supplementary admission window, apply to another institution, use your current JAMB score for direct applications, or re-sit JAMB the following year. We guide you through all these options.

How do I know if I have been admitted?
+

You can check your admission status through the JAMB CAPS portal (caps.jamb.gov.ng) or your institution's own admission portal. We can check this for you, notify you of your status and guide you on what to do next.

Can I change my course or institution after JAMB?
+

Yes, JAMB allows candidates to change their institution and/or course choice through the CAPS portal — usually before the deadline for Post-UTME. We handle this process for you to ensure it's done correctly and before deadlines.
